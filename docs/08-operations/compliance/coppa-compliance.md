# COPPA Compliance Documentation

## 1. COPPA Overview

### 1.1 What is COPPA?
The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) is a federal law that protects the privacy of children under 13 years of age. COPPA requires operators of websites and online services to obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting, using, or disclosing personal information from children under 13.

### 1.2 COPPA Requirements
- **Verifiable Parental Consent**: Must obtain consent before collecting data
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only necessary information
- **Data Security**: Implement reasonable security measures
- **Data Retention**: Retain data only as long as necessary
- **Parental Rights**: Provide parents with access, deletion, and opt-out rights
- **Privacy Notice**: Clear, understandable privacy notices

### 1.3 Applicability to ToolboxAI Solutions
Our platform serves educational institutions and may collect data from children under 13, making COPPA compliance essential for:
- Student accounts and profiles
- Educational progress tracking
- Learning analytics and assessments
- Communication features
- Roblox integration and gameplay data

## 4. Parental Consent Management

### 4.1 Consent Collection Methods

#### 4.1.1 Verifiable Parental Consent
We use multiple methods to obtain verifiable parental consent:

**Email Verification**:
- Send consent request to parent's email
- Require parent to click verification link
- Confirm parent's identity through additional verification

**Phone Verification**:
- Call parent's phone number
- Verify identity through security questions
- Confirm consent verbally and in writing

**Video Call Verification**:
- Schedule video call with parent
- Verify identity with government-issued ID
- Record consent confirmation

**Digital Signature**:
- Electronic signature with timestamp
- IP address and device fingerprinting
- Multi-factor authentication

#### 4.1.2 Consent Documentation
- **Consent Records**: Detailed records of all consent obtained
